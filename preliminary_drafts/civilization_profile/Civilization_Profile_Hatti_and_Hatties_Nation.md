# Civilization Profile – Hatti's Land and Hatties Nation

## I. Name Identity

- **Land / Region Name:** Land of Hatti or Anatolia (Modern Era)
- **Capital | Population Name:** Hattusa | Hatties
- **Era:** Old Era  
- **Elemental Alignment:** Fire & Earth  
- **Geographic Location:** Continental mountain network overlooking vast plains and forests  
- **Symbol / Emblem:** A hammer striking an anvil surrounded by a mountain ring *
- **National Motto:** “Stone Endures. Fire Transforms.” *
- **Cultural Inspiration:** Mostly Hittite culture (for metallurgy  & traditions) and Spartan warrior culture inside a social class. 

( * = To be refined later)

## II. Geography & Environment

- **Terrain Type:** Dense mineral-rich mountain network bordered by Vast plains and forests.  
- **Climate:** Temperate but harsh; long winters in high altitude areas and dry winds across the plains  
- **Importants Sites:** 
  - Hattusa, the Great Fortress City built along the mountain slope 
  - The Deep Forge Labyrinth of Kumarbi
  - Mountainous massif of Huwasi
- **Natural Borders:** Mountain network forming a natural barrier between the plains and Hatties people  
- **Natural Resources:**
  - Iron and other metal ores  
  - Stone and rare minerals  
  - Geothermal heat sources within the mountains  
- **Flora & Fauna:**
  - Dense hunting forests  
  - Wide plains inhabited by dangerous beasts and monsters  
  - Sparse vegetation in rocky mountain regions  
- **Strategic Advantages:**
  - Abundant mineral resources  
  - Fortress city protected by mountainous terrain  
  - Highly developed weapon and armor craftsmanship  
- **Strategic Weaknesses:**
  - Constant threat from powerful fauna and monsters  
  - Limited agricultural land due to rocky terrain  
  - Dependence on mining infrastructure for survival  

## III. Society & Social Structure

- **Legend Leader / King:** The Master Smith, Murs Hasamili
- **Society System:** Dual-layer society divided between surface city and underground forge network  
- **Political System:** Centralized warrior monarchy supported by respected craft leadership  
- **Class Structure:** 
  - **The Fortress's King:**  Supreme protector and military leader  
  - **Master Smith:** Leader of the forge communities and guardian of craft knowledge  
  - **Warriors:** Hunters and defenders of the plains and forests  
  - **Miners & Smiths:** Extract resources and produce weapons, armor, and tools  
  - **Commoners:** Farmers, traders, builders, and families of the fortress city  
- **Social Hierarchy:** Respect based on contribution to survival: strength, protection, and craftsmanship  
- **Military Presence:** Strong warrior class trained to hunt monsters and defend the city  
- **Education System:** 
  - Depending of the Child's innate talent
  - Gifted children oriented to high social class (Warriors or Smiths) 
  - Medium mastery oriented to city support (defence, mining, civil constructions)  
  - Poor level pushed to be commoners  
- **Justice System:** 
  - Honor-based justice  
  - Crimes judged by leaders and warrior councils  
  - Emphasis on responsibility and contribution to the community  

## IV. Culture & Lifestyle

- **Core Values:** Strength, Endurance, Discipline, Craftsmanship  
- **View on Family:** Strong family loyalty within fortress districts and mining clans  
- **View on Honor:** Protecting the people and contributing to the survival of the nation  
- **View on Nature:** Nature is hostile and must be endured or shaped for survival  

### Daily Life of a Commoner

- Hunting expeditions in plains and forests  
- Training in weapon use  
- Mining expeditions in mountain tunnels  
- Crafting tools, weapons, and defensive structures  

### Architecture Style

- Massive stone fortifications  
- Reinforced iron gates and towers  
- Functional and durable designs built to resist attack  

### Food Culture

- Meat-based diet from hunting  
- Preserved food for long winters  
- Root vegetables and gathered forest plants  

### Festivals / Rituals

- Great Forge Festival celebrating master craftsmanship *
- Victory Hunts honoring successful monster hunts *
- Warrior Ascension Trial for young fighters *

### Funeral Traditions

- Warriors honored with weapon burials  
- Ashes sometimes placed within the mountain stone  
- Symbolizing return to earth and fire  

## V. Elemental Doctrine

- **Who Can Use the Element?** Elemental ability exists in parts of the population; warriors and smiths may train in its use  

### Training Method

- Physical discipline and endurance training  
- Meditation near volcanic vents or deep stone chambers  
- Mastery through practical use during forging or combat  

### Limits of Power

- Excessive fire usage may cause destructive loss of control  
- Excessive earth usage may lead to physical exhaustion or rigidity  

### Philosophy Behind the Element

- Earth represents endurance, structure, and protection  
- Fire represents transformation, creation, and power  

Together they symbolize the nation's belief:

Strength protects. Craft transforms.

### Forbidden Techniques

- Using fire to destroy the fortress itself  
- Collapsing sacred mining tunnels or destabilizing the mountains  

### Relationship Between Element & Identity

Citizens often reflect their elemental affinity:

- Warriors lean toward fire  
- Builders and miners lean toward earth  

## VI. Economy & Technology

- **Main Economic Activity:** Mining, metallurgy, weapon forging, monster hunting  
- **Trade Relations:** Limited trade with neighboring civilizations; resources mostly used internally  
- **Craft Specialization:** Weapon forging, armor crafting, stone engineering  
- **Medical Knowledge:** Primitive battlefield medicine and survival treatments  
- **Technological Level Compared to Others:** Advanced metallurgy and engineering, low to moderate agricultural development

- **Sacred Figures:** 
  - The Mountain God **Huwasi**
  - The Hunter God **Kurunta**
- **Creation Myth:** The mountains were once sleeping giants whose bones became the foundation of the land *
- **End-of-World Belief:** The world ends when the mountains break and the forges go silent *

## VIII. Relationships With Other Civilizations

- **Allies:** Rare alliances based on mutual strength or resource exchange  
- **Enemies:** Predominantly monstrous creatures inhabiting the plains and wilderness  
- **Historical Conflicts:** Continuous battles against dangerous fauna rather than other civilizations  
- **Current Tension Level:** Potential rivalry with civilizations that challenge their strength  

---

## IX. Narrative Role in the Story

- **Old Era Role:** Military and industrial powerhouse of the ancient world  
- **Modern Era Influence:** Source of legendary weapons, lost metallurgy, and fortress architecture  
- **Philosophical Opposition To:** Nature-harmony civilizations like Emain Ablach  
- **What They Represent Symbolically:** The power of strength, discipline, and creation

## X. Historical Chronicle of the Land

According to ancient tales, the first settlers arrived in the region seeking refuge from the monstrous beasts roaming the plains.

At first they lived in scattered family groups hiding among the mountains.

Repeated attacks forced them to unite.

Warriors gathered the people and constructed the first stone fortress along the mountain front, creating a defensive stronghold overlooking the plains.

Soon after, miners discovered immense mineral wealth beneath the mountains.

A legendary smith forged the first weapons capable of defeating the monstrous creatures threatening the people.

From that moment, the civilization of Fire & Earth was born.

A nation where the sword defends the people, and the forge gives the sword its strength.
