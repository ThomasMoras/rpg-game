# Design Pillars (TO REFINE)

These pillars define the **core philosophy** of the game and guide all design decisions.

---

## 1. Tactical Positioning Matters

Combat revolves around **positioning on a 3D grid**.

Players must think about:

* movement range
* terrain elevation
* line of sight
* enemy positioning

Small maps ensure encounters remain **intense and readable**.

---

## 2. Environmental Interaction

The battlefield is not static.

Examples:

* fire spreading from burning objects
* destructible elements
* environmental hazards

Example interactions:

* Fireball ignites a tree → surrounding tiles catch fire
* Heavy object hits tree → damage or destruction

Environment becomes part of the strategy.

---

## 3. Fast Tactical Combat

Encounters are designed to be **short and engaging**.

Features supporting this:

* small combat arenas
* limited movement ranges
* clear turn order

A **turn timer system** may push combat forward if the player delays too long.

---

## 4. Clear Tactical Feedback

The player must always understand:

* movement possibilities
* attack ranges
* ability effects
* turn order

Visual systems such as:

* grid highlighting
* path previews
* damage indicators
* status effects

ensure combat decisions remain clear.

---

## 5. Expandable Systems

All gameplay systems are designed to be **data-driven and modular**, allowing:

* new abilities
* new characters
* new combat mechanics
* additional environments
